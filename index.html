Frequently Asked Questions

A Lucky 15 is a full-cover bet consisting of 15 individual bets across 4 selections: 4 singles, 6 doubles, 4 trebles, and 1 four-fold accumulator. You only need 1 winner to get a return, but the more winners you have, the greater your profit.

An Each Way bet is actually two bets: one for the selection to win and one for it to place (finish in the top 2, 3, 4 or 5 positions, depending on the race). The place part pays out at a fraction of the win odds (commonly 1/4 or 1/5).

A Rule 4 deduction applies when a horse is withdrawn from a race after you've placed your bet. Because the non-runner improves the chances of remaining horses, bookmakers reduce your winnings by a percentage (5p to 75p per £1 won). Enable Rule 4 in the calculator to account for this.

If a selection is void or a non-runner, it is simply removed from each combination it was part of. For example, in a double, it becomes a single. In a treble, it becomes a double. Your stake for the affected bets is returned.

Stake Per Bet: The amount you wager on each individual line. A £1 Lucky 15 stake per bet costs £15 total (15 × £1).
Total Stake: The total amount you wish to spend. This is divided equally across all bets. A £15 total stake on a Lucky 15 means £1 per bet.
