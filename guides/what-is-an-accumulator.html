What is an Accumulator Bet?

The accumulator — or "acca" — is the UK's most popular multiple bet. All selections must win, but the potential returns are enormous.

Accumulator Basics

An accumulator (or "acca") is a single bet on 4 or more selections, all of which must win. The return from each winning selection rolls over and becomes the stake for the next leg, creating a powerful multiplier effect.

Key fact: A 4-fold accumulator with all selections at 2/1 (3.0) would return 81× your stake. A £2 acca would return £162. 
Accumulator Return = Stake × (Odds_1 × Odds_2 × ... × Odds_n)

4-fold example: £5 @ 2/1, 3/1, 4/1, 5/1
= £5 × 3.0 × 4.0 × 5.0 × 6.0
= £5 × 360 = £1,800
Profit = £1,800 − £5 = £1,795

What Happens if a Leg is Void?

If any selection in your accumulator is void (postponed, cancelled, or a non-runner), that leg is simply removed from the accumulator. Your bet then becomes a shorter accumulator on the remaining legs:

  • 5-fold with 1 void → 4-fold
  • 4-fold with 1 void → Treble
  • Treble with 1 void → Double

Your potential return will be recalculated based on the remaining odds.

Accumulator Bonuses

Many bookmakers offer Acca Boost or accumulator bonuses. These add a percentage on top of your winnings when all legs win:

  • 4-fold boost: 5-10% extra
  • 5-fold boost: 10-15% extra
  • 6+ fold boost: 15-50% extra (varies by bookmaker)
