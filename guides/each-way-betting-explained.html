Each Way Betting Explained

An each-way bet is two bets in one — win and place. This guide explains how it works, the place terms, and how to calculate your returns.

What is an Each Way Bet?

An each-way (E/W) bet is actually two separate bets of equal stake:

  1. The Win bet — your selection must win at full odds.
  2. The Place bet — your selection must finish within the place positions at a fraction of the win odds.

Because it's two bets, an each-way stake doubles your outlay. A £5 each-way bet costs £10 in total (£5 win + £5 place).

Each Way Place Terms

The place fraction and number of places paid varies by race type and bookmaker:

Race Type / RunnersPlaces PaidOdds Fraction
2–4 runners (non-handicap)Win onlyN/A
5–7 runners2 places1/4
8–11 runners3 places1/5 (or 1/4)
12–15 runners3 places1/4
16+ runners (handicap)4 places1/4
Grand National5–7 places*1/4 or 1/5

*Varies by bookmaker. Enhanced place terms available during big racing festivals.

Each Way Return Calculation

Win Return = Win Stake × Win Odds (decimal)
Place Return = Place Stake × (1 + (Win Odds − 1) × Place Fraction)

Example: £5 E/W @ 10/1 (11.0), 1/4 odds, 4 places
Win Return (if wins): £5 × 11.0 = £55.00
Place Return (if places): £5 × (1 + 10 × 0.25) = £5 × 3.5 = £17.50

If WINS: Total = £55.00 + £17.50 = £72.50 (profit = £62.50)
If PLACED (2nd/3rd/4th): Total = £17.50 (win bet loses: −£5, net = +£7.50)
If LOSES: Total = £0 (lose both bets = −£10)

FAQ

Each-way betting is most valuable with outsiders at longer odds in large fields. If you back a 20/1 shot each-way and it places, you still make a profit on the place part even though the win bet loses.

If your selection ties for a place position with other runners, a dead heat rule applies. Your place stake is divided by the number of horses in the dead heat, and you receive a reduced place return.

Each-way betting is primarily designed for horse racing and some golf markets. It is not commonly available for football match results, though some tournament betting markets (e.g. top scorer, tournament winner) may offer each-way terms.
