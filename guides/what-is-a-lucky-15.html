What is a Lucky 15 Bet?

A Lucky 15 is a full-cover bet that consists of 15 individual bets across 4 selections in different events. It includes every possible combination of those 4 selections from singles all the way up to a four-fold accumulator.

The "Lucky" part of the name comes from the bookmaker bonuses that were traditionally attached to these bets. The number 15 refers to the total number of individual bets placed.

Key fact: With a Lucky 15, you only need 1 winner to get a return. This is what makes it one of the most popular horse racing bets in the UK.

A £1 Lucky 15 costs £15 in total (15 bets × £1 each). A 50p Lucky 15 costs £7.50. An each-way Lucky 15 at £1 per bet costs £30 (30 bets).

The 15 Bets Explained

A Lucky 15 across selections A, B, C, and D contains the following 15 bets:

Bet TypeNumberCombinations
Singles4A, B, C, D
Doubles6AB, AC, AD, BC, BD, CD
Trebles4ABC, ABD, ACD, BCD
Four-fold1ABCD
Total15

This structure ensures that every possible outcome is covered. Even if three of your four selections lose, the single on the one winning selection still returns something.

How to Calculate Lucky 15 Returns

Manually calculating a Lucky 15 requires working out 15 individual bets and adding the returns together. Here's a worked example:

Example: £1 Lucky 15 (stake per bet, total cost £15)
Selection A: Won at 3/1 (4.0 decimal)
Selection B: Won at 2/1 (3.0 decimal)
Selection C: Lost
Selection D: Lost 
Singles that win:
  A: £1 × 4.0 = £4.00
  B: £1 × 3.0 = £3.00

Doubles that win (both legs must win):
  AB: £1 × 4.0 × 3.0 = £12.00
  AC: Lost (C lost)
  AD: Lost (D lost)
  BC: Lost (C lost)
  BD: Lost (D lost)
  CD: Lost

Trebles that win: all contain C or D — all lost

Four-fold: contains C and D — lost

Total Return = £4.00 + £3.00 + £12.00 = £19.00
Total Outlay = £15.00
Total Profit = £4.00

Each Way Lucky 15

An each-way Lucky 15 doubles the number of bets to 30 — 15 win bets and 15 place bets. This also doubles the total outlay: a £1 each-way Lucky 15 costs £30.

The place part of each bet pays out at a fraction of the win odds (typically 1/4 or 1/5 of the odds) if the selection finishes within the place positions (usually 2nd or 3rd, sometimes 4th or 5th in large fields).

Each-way Lucky 15s are extremely popular in horse racing, where places can be very lucrative in big handicap races.

Tip: For major horse racing events like the Cheltenham Festival or Grand National, many bookmakers offer enhanced place terms (e.g. 1/5 odds, 5 places) on each-way bets. Always check the exact terms before placing.

Non-Runners and Void Selections

If one of your Lucky 15 selections is a non-runner (a horse withdrawn before the race) or the event is void, that selection is treated as if it doesn't exist. The affected bets are reduced:

  • A double containing the void selection becomes a single
  • A treble containing the void selection becomes a double
  • The four-fold becomes a treble
  • The single on the void selection is a push (stake returned)

The calculator handles non-runners automatically — simply set the outcome to "Void / N-R".

When Should You Place a Lucky 15?

A Lucky 15 is best suited for punters who:

  1. Have 4 horse racing selections they fancy, but don't want to put all their eggs in one basket with a straight four-fold accumulator.
  2. Want a safety net — even if only 1 or 2 selections win, you still get something back.
  3. Are looking to take advantage of bookmaker bonuses (e.g. double odds on a single winner).
  4. Are placing each-way bets on horses with each-way places available — the Lucky 15 structure maximises returns from placed horses.
Remember: A Lucky 15 costs 15 times your unit stake. If you normally bet £5 on a single, a £5 Lucky 15 would cost £75 total. Consider starting with smaller stakes like 10p or 25p per bet.

Lucky 15 FAQ

A 50p Lucky 15 costs £7.50 in total (15 × £0.50).

Lucky 15s are most popular in horse racing and greyhound racing, but can be placed on virtually any sport: football, golf, tennis, cricket, and more. However, the bookmaker bonuses (double odds, etc.) typically apply to horse racing selections only.

A Lucky 15 provides much more security than a four-fold accumulator. With a four-fold, all 4 must win for any return — if one loses, you get nothing. With a Lucky 15, just one winner gives a return. The trade-off is cost: a Lucky 15 costs 15× your unit stake vs 1× for the accumulator.
