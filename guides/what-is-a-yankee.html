What is a Yankee Bet?

A Yankee is a 4-selection, 11-bet wager covering all doubles, trebles and a four-fold. You need just 2 winners for a return.

Yankee Bet Overview

A Yankee is one of the most popular multiple bets in UK horse racing. It consists of 11 bets across 4 selections:

Bet TypeNumber of BetsMinimum Winners Required
Doubles62
Trebles43
Four-fold Accumulator14
Total112 (for any return)

A £1 Yankee costs £11 in total. You need at least 2 winners for any return (from the doubles).

Yankee vs Lucky 15

The Yankee and Lucky 15 both cover 4 selections, but with one key difference:

A Lucky 15 adds 4 singles to the 11 Yankee bets, giving 15 bets total. This means a single winner returns something in a Lucky 15, but you need 2 winners in a Yankee.

Worked Example

£1 Yankee example (cost: £11)
Horse A: Won @ 4/1 (5.0)
Horse B: Won @ 3/1 (4.0)
Horse C: Won @ 2/1 (3.0)
Horse D: Lost 
Doubles (AB, AC, BC win; AD, BD, CD contain D — lost):
  AB: £1 × 5.0 × 4.0 = £20.00
  AC: £1 × 5.0 × 3.0 = £15.00
  BC: £1 × 4.0 × 3.0 = £12.00
  AD, BD, CD: £0.00 each (D lost)

Trebles (ABC wins; ABD, ACD, BCD contain D — lost):
  ABC: £1 × 5.0 × 4.0 × 3.0 = £60.00
  ABD, ACD, BCD: £0.00 (D lost)

Four-fold (contains D — lost): £0.00

Total Return = £20.00 + £15.00 + £12.00 + £60.00 = £107.00
Total Outlay = £11.00
Total Profit = £96.00
